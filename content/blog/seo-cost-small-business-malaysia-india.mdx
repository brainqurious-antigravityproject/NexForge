The honest answer nobody gives you upfront: it depends on who's doing it, what they're doing, and whether what they're doing actually works.

SEO for a small business in Malaysia or India ranges from RM 300 to RM 3,000 per month (roughly ₹6,000 to ₹60,000), depending on scope and provider. The difference between the bottom and top of that range is not just quality — it's whether the work compounds into lasting rankings or disappears the moment you stop paying.

Here's what the money actually buys at each level.

## The Three Tiers of SEO (and What Each Delivers)

### Tier 1: DIY SEO (RM 0–300/month | ₹0–5,000/month)

Cost: your time plus basic tools (Google Search Console is free; tools like Ubersuggest or Ahrefs Lite run RM 100–250/month).

What you can do yourself:

- Submit your site to Google via Search Console
- Set up and optimise your Google Business Profile
- Add proper page titles and meta descriptions to every page
- Publish one well-structured blog post per month targeting a specific keyword
- Get listed in local directories (JomDirectory, Yelp India, Justdial, etc.)

What you can't do without experience: competitive keyword research, technical SEO audits, link building, and tracking whether any of it is working.

**Best for:** Businesses with limited budgets who are willing to invest consistent time. Results are slow but real if you're disciplined.

### Tier 2: Affordable Agency or Freelancer (RM 300–900/month | ₹6,000–18,000/month)

This is the most common tier — and the most variable in quality.

At this price point, you're likely getting:

- Monthly keyword tracking
- Basic on-page optimisation (titles, headings, meta descriptions)
- Some content production (1–2 blog posts or page updates per month)
- A monthly report showing rankings and traffic

What you may not be getting: genuine link building, technical SEO fixes, strategy that evolves with your business, or someone who understands your specific market.

The real risk at this tier: many providers charge for activity, not results. Monthly reports showing keyword positions moving up and down without any explanation of why — or what's being done about it.

**Best for:** Businesses that want SEO handled without doing it themselves, and have patience to vet providers carefully and ask for case studies before signing.

### Tier 3: Proper SEO Engagement (RM 1,000–3,000+/month | ₹20,000–60,000+/month)

At this level, you're paying for:

- Comprehensive technical audit and fixes (page speed, indexability, schema markup)
- Competitive keyword research tied to your specific services and geography
- Content strategy with regular publication
- Link building through genuine outreach and directory placements
- Monthly strategy calls, not just reports
- Adjustments based on what Google is actually responding to

**Best for:** Established businesses ready to treat SEO as a channel, not an experiment.

## What Does SEO Actually Cost in Malaysia vs. India?

| | Malaysia | India |
|---|---|---|
| **DIY tools (optional)** | RM 0–250/month | ₹0–5,000/month |
| **Affordable freelancer/agency** | RM 300–900/month | ₹6,000–18,000/month |
| **Mid-tier agency** | RM 1,000–2,000/month | ₹20,000–40,000/month |
| **Full-service SEO engagement** | RM 2,000–5,000+/month | ₹40,000–100,000+/month |

Local and niche-specific SEO (e.g. "wedding photographer Johor Bahru") costs less and delivers results faster than broad national terms. Most small businesses should start local and specific.

## Is SEO Worth It for a Small Business?

Yes — with conditions.

**SEO is worth it when:**

- Your customers search for what you offer before buying
- You can sustain the investment for at least six months
- The work is done correctly from the start (bad SEO can actively hurt your rankings)

**SEO is not worth it when:**

- Your business is referral-only and search is not how clients find you
- You need customers this week — SEO works on a months-long timeline
- You hire the cheapest provider, get no results, and conclude "SEO doesn't work"

The last point is the most common trap. SEO that doesn't work still costs money. The difference between RM 400/month in wasted spend and RM 800/month in genuine results is not the price — it's whether the work is actually being done.
