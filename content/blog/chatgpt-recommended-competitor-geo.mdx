Someone asked ChatGPT for a web designer in Malaysia. ChatGPT named three agencies. Yours wasn't one of them.

This is happening to small businesses across Malaysia and India right now — and most of them don't know it. They're watching their Google rankings, adjusting their ads, and updating their Instagram. Meanwhile, a growing number of potential clients are bypassing Google entirely, asking AI assistants for recommendations, and getting answers that don't include them.

[GEO (Generative Engine Optimization)](/blog/what-is-geo-generative-engine-optimization) is the practice of making your website and brand visible inside the responses generated by large language models.

## Why AI Assistants Recommend Some Businesses and Not Others

ChatGPT doesn't have a business directory. It doesn't pull from a live database of local companies. It generates answers based on patterns learned from a massive volume of web content — articles, directories, reviews, forum discussions, and websites.

A business that gets recommended by AI tools tends to have clear, structured content, consistent business information across the web, and mentions in credible third-party sources.

## How to Audit Your Current AI Visibility

Before fixing the problem, you need to understand the gap. The fastest way to audit your AI visibility is to ask the systems themselves. Open ChatGPT or Perplexity and try these prompts:

- "Can you recommend a [your service] in [your city]?"
- "Tell me about [your business name] in [your city]."
- "How does [your business name] compare to [competitor name] for [specific service]?"

If the AI cannot find you, or if it gives a vague answer while giving your competitor a detailed recommendation, you have an "entity signal" gap.

![AI visibility audit prompt example — asking ChatGPT to compare your business versus a competitor to identify entity signal gaps](/chatgptrecommendmycompetitor/ai-visibility-audit-chatgpt-competitor-comparison-prompt.webp)

## Six Steps to Make AI Recommend Your Business

To bridge the gap and ensure AI assistants can confidently recommend your business, you need to implement several key fixes:

1. **Plain Language Headlines:** State exactly what you do and who you serve on your homepage.
2. **Schema Markup:** Add LocalBusiness structured data so AI systems have machine-readable proof of your identity.
3. **NAP Consistency:** Ensure your Name, Address, and Phone number are identical across every platform.
4. **Citations & Mentions:** Get listed in credible local and industry directories.
5. **Customer Reviews:** Build a footprint of verified customer sentiment on Google and other platforms.
6. **Specific, Answer-Based Content:** Publish [articles that directly answer the questions your customers are asking](/blog/what-is-aeo-answer-engine-optimization).

## Closing the AI Recommendation Gap

The difference between being invisible to AI and being recommended is the strength of your "entity signals." A business with weak signals is ignored; a business with strong, consistent signals becomes the AI's default recommendation.
