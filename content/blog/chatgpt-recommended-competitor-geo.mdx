Someone asked ChatGPT for a web designer in Malaysia. ChatGPT named three agencies. Yours wasn't one of them.

This is happening to small businesses across Malaysia and India right now — and most of them don't know it. They're watching their Google rankings, adjusting their ads, and updating their Instagram. Meanwhile, a growing number of potential clients are bypassing Google entirely, asking AI assistants for recommendations, and getting answers that don't include them.

The discipline that determines whether ChatGPT, Perplexity, or Gemini names your business is called GEO — Generative Engine Optimization. Here's what it is, why your competitor may already be doing it (deliberately or accidentally), and what you can do about it.

## Why AI Assistants Recommend Some Businesses and Not Others

ChatGPT doesn't have a business directory. It doesn't pull from a live database of local companies. It generates answers based on patterns learned from a massive volume of web content — articles, directories, reviews, forum discussions, and websites.

A business that gets recommended by AI tools tends to have several things in common:

- Its website clearly states what it does, who it serves, and where it operates
- It's mentioned consistently across multiple credible sources (directories, reviews, articles)
- Its content answers real questions in clear, structured language that AI can extract
- Its business information is consistent everywhere it appears online

None of this requires a large budget. It requires deliberate, consistent content and presence-building — which is exactly what most small businesses skip.

## 7 Specific Things That Make AI Recommend Your Business

### 1. State What You Do in Plain Language — Immediately

AI systems read your homepage and try to classify your business. If your homepage says "We create extraordinary digital experiences for forward-thinking brands," the AI cannot confidently recommend you for anything specific.

If it says "Sitecraf builds websites for small businesses in Malaysia and India — landing pages, business sites, and e-commerce stores with ongoing care included," the AI has everything it needs to match you to a relevant query.

**Action:** Check your homepage headline and your About page. Can an AI read them and immediately know: what you sell, who you sell it to, and where you operate?

### 2. Publish Content That Answers Real Questions

AI assistants generate answers by drawing on content that answers questions clearly. A business that publishes detailed, well-structured answers to the questions its customers ask is, over time, more likely to be the source those answers are drawn from.

A web agency that publishes "How much does a business website cost in Malaysia?" and answers it clearly is more likely to be cited when someone asks ChatGPT the same question than an agency whose website has no content at all.

**Action:** Publish at least two to four blog posts per month that directly answer questions your clients ask. Structure them with clear headings, concise definitions, and direct answers before explanations.

### 3. Be Consistent Across Every Platform

AI systems cross-reference information. If your business name, address, phone number, and service description vary across your website, Google Business Profile, Facebook page, and directory listings — that inconsistency reduces confidence in your data.

A business with clean, consistent information across ten sources is more citeable than one with conflicting details across three.

**Action:** Audit every place your business appears online. Standardise your business name, address, phone number, and service description. Exact consistency signals credibility to AI systems.

### 4. Get Mentioned on Third-Party Sites

Your own website saying you're a good web agency is expected. A directory, a review platform, an industry association, or a publication saying it carries more weight — for both Google and AI systems.

Third-party mentions tell AI models: other sources have verified this business exists and is relevant to this category.

**Action:** Get listed in relevant local directories (Google Business Profile, Clutch, Justdial, local chamber directories). Ask satisfied clients for reviews on Google. Seek mentions in relevant publications, even small ones.

### 5. Use Structured Data (Schema Markup)

Schema markup is code added to your website that explicitly tells search engines and AI systems: "this is a local business, this is what it does, this is where it operates, this is how to contact it."

Most small business websites don't have it. Adding it gives AI systems machine-readable confirmation of your business identity — the difference between a system inferring what you do and knowing it with confidence.

**Action:** Ask your web developer to add LocalBusiness schema markup to your website. If you're on WordPress or a modern site builder, plugins handle this. It takes under an hour to implement correctly.

### 6. Write Definitions That AI Can Extract

AI assistants love clearly defined terms. A page that clearly defines "what is website care" or "what is AEO" in the first sentence of a section is easier to cite than a page that meanders around the definition for three paragraphs.

**Action:** For every important concept on your site, write a one-sentence definition that appears at the start of the relevant section. "Website care is an ongoing service that handles backups, security updates, bug fixes, and content edits for small business websites." That's extractable. An AI can use it.

### 7. Build Content Volume Over Time

AI systems are trained on large volumes of content. A business with twenty well-structured articles, a comprehensive services page, and consistent third-party mentions has a much larger footprint in the training data — and therefore a higher probability of appearing in generated answers — than a business with a two-page website.

**Action:** Treat content as a long-term asset. Twelve well-structured articles published over a year compound into a GEO signal that a one-page site will never match.

## How Long Does This Take to Work?

GEO is not immediate. AI models are updated periodically, not in real time. But the signals you build now — consistent mentions, structured content, schema markup, authoritative third-party links — accumulate and are reflected in future model updates and real-time retrieval tools like Perplexity.

The businesses being recommended by AI tools today are the ones that started building their content presence twelve to twenty-four months ago. The businesses that start now will be the ones recommended twelve months from now.
